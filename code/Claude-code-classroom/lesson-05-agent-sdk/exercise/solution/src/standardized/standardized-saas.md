## Contract Summary

### Parties
- **Vendor:** CloudTech Solutions Inc.
- **Customer:** Acme Corporation

### Term
- **Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
- **Duration:** 12 months

### Financial Terms
- **Payment Amount:** $2,500 per month ($30,000 per year)
- **Payment Frequency:** Billed annually in advance
- **Payment Schedule:** Payment due within 30 days of invoice

### Legal Terms
- **Liability Cap:** Provider's total liability shall not exceed fees paid in the prior 12 months. Neither party liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages.
- **Termination Notice:** 60 days written notice required by either party
- **Termination Conditions:**
  - Either party may terminate with 60 days written notice
  - Customer may terminate immediately if Provider breaches and fails to cure within 30 days

### IP & Data
- **IP Ownership:** Provider retains all IP rights to the platform. Customer retains rights to their data.
- **Data Protection:**
  - Data stored in US data centers
  - Customer retains ownership of all data
  - Provider will delete data within 30 days of termination upon request
- **Confidentiality:** Both parties agree to maintain confidentiality of proprietary information for 3 years following termination

### Special Provisions
- Cloud-based project management platform service
- 30-day cure period for Provider breaches before Customer can terminate immediately
- Data deletion available upon request within 30 days of termination

### Risk Assessment
- **Missing Terms:**
  - No auto-renewal clause specified (unclear what happens after 12 months)
  - No Service Level Agreement (SLA) or uptime guarantees defined
  - No disaster recovery or business continuity provisions
  - No data breach notification requirements
  - No limitations on price increases for renewal periods
  - No force majeure clause
  - No dispute resolution or arbitration procedures
  - No governing law or jurisdiction specified
- **Potential Red Flags:**
  - Annual advance payment ($30,000) creates significant financial risk if service quality issues arise
  - No refund policy specified for early termination or service failures
  - Liability cap may be inadequate for data loss or security breach scenarios
  - No specific compliance standards mentioned (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, etc.)
  - Broad confidentiality obligation without clear definition of what constitutes proprietary information
