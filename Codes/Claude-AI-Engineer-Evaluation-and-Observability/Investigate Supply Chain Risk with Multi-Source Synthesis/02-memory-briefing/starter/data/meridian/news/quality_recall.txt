Electronics supplier Meridian Components issued a limited quality advisory in the first quarter of 2026 after internal testing flagged a potential reliability concern in a specific lot of its surface-mount capacitor assemblies. The advisory, circulated to affected customers in February 2026, covers a single production batch manufactured in late December 2025 that may exhibit elevated leakage current under sustained thermal stress. Meridian Components characterized the action as a precautionary, voluntary recall rather than a safety-driven mandate, emphasizing that no field failures had resulted in customer-end product damage. The company estimated that fewer than 40,000 units across roughly a dozen customer accounts fell within the affected date code range and offered free replacement stock plus expedited shipping. Quality engineers at Meridian Components attributed the issue to a drift in a dielectric curing parameter on one production line, which has since been corrected and revalidated. A company quality director said enhanced lot traceability and additional in-line thermal screening had been implemented to prevent recurrence. Several customers acknowledged the prompt notification, though one contract manufacturer said the disruption forced a brief reinspection of incoming inventory before resuming its own assembly lines.
